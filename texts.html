Sans Serif

Sans serif fonts, such as Arial and Helvetica, are known for their clean and modern look, lacking the small decorative strokes at the ends of letters. They are widely used for digital media due to their readability on screens.

Serif Font

Serif fonts, like Times New Roman and Garamond, feature small lines or strokes attached to the ends of letters, giving a more traditional and formal appearance. These fonts are often used in print media for their elegant, stylish and classic look.

Monospace

Monospace fonts, such as Courier New and Consolas, have characters that occupy the same horizontal space. This uniform spacing makes them ideal for coding, as they enhance readability.
